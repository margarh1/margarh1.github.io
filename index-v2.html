A Little Bit About Me

I started in biology, Ecology and Evolutionary Biology to be exact, and aimed to go into research upon graduation. During my undergraduate career, I managed to gain a position as a lab assistant in a research lab and found that, while I enjoyed the atmosphere of the lab, I could not find any passion for the research itself.

Upon graduation, I moved back to the San Francisco Bay Area, which disconnected me from the network I was only just beginning to build in Irvine, and found my way to programming. I had taken a few of the introductory classes in college and found, to my surprise, that there was a lot less math than I had originally expected. It also turned out to be a lot simpler to understand than I expected and so, I turned to programming.

Over the course of my time at General Assembly, one of many coding bootcamps found in the San Francisco Bay Area, I realized that the things that I had loved about science in the first place--problem solving and thinking critically about the problems and situations given to you--could also be found in programming. I also managed to figure out that the problem I had had with biological research was the amount of time it required before a given problem could be solved, a problem that was much less prevalent in programming.

I recently completed my course at General Assembly and am looking for a job that will allow me to continue solving problems, to think critically and creatively about ways to solve a given problem, and to continue learning new technologies. In the meantime, I have projects I am working on streamlining, a language to brush up on, and ideas for additional applications that I will hopefully be able to start in the near future.
